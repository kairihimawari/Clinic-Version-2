How to Use Abortion Pills

How to take the pills

Day 1: Swallow one mifepristone tablet with water.

Day 2 (at least 24 hours later):

  1. Take a pain reliever (e.g., ibuprofen, naproxen; acetaminophen if you can‚Äôt take NSAIDs).
  2. Place 4 misoprostol tablets under the tongue or between gum and cheek. Let dissolve for 30 minutes, then swallow fragments.
  3. After 3 hours, take 2 more misoprostol tablets the same way.
  4. After another 3 hours, repeat with 2 more misoprostol tablets.

If bleeding started earlier, continue all steps. Keep any remaining tablets unless a clinician tells you otherwise.

What to expect

  • Bleeding & cramps usually start after misoprostol. Heavier than a period, may include clots.
  • It may take up to 24 hours after the last misoprostol dose for bleeding to begin.
  • Pain varies; strong cramps are common.
  • Bleeding slows once pregnancy passes, then may come and go until your next period (4‚Äì8 weeks).

Pain & comfort tips

  • Use over-the-counter pain meds as needed.
  • Heat (heating pad, hot water bottle), rest, music, supportive company help.

Managing nausea

  • Small, frequent snacks and fluids help.
  • Some use doxylamine, dimenhydrinate, bismuth subsalicylate, ginger, or vitamin B6.

After the abortion

  • If bleeding/cramping happened and symptoms fade within 2 weeks, no routine follow-up needed.
  • Home pregnancy tests are most reliable at 5 weeks after pills.
  • Resume activities, including sex, when ready. Pregnancy possible as soon as 8 days after abortion.
